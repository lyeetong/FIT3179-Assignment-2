Global Mortality

Mortality has always been a sober issue of concern globally. Not only does it reflect the social condition and social development of a country, it is more vital in reflecting a country's citizens' health status. Mortality is usually picturized as connected with health problems and accidents. However, consequences brought by poor diet and unhealthy physical practices shall not be neglected and they are our focus in this visualisation.

Death Risk Factors

Overall, physical practices generally have greater impact compared to diet practices in causing deaths. Out of the 5 physical practices, smoking has the highest mortality count. The consequence of smoking is well-known, for it is able to increase the risk of stroke and coronary disease, which are the key cause of mortality from the health perspective. However, the danger of the second top-scoring unsafe sex is usually neglected when in fact, the infectious disease brought along during the sexual transmission can be fatal. On the other hand, all 5 diet practices have a similar impact overall with diet low in whole grains slightly outweighs other diet practices.

Diet vs Physical Practices

Diet practices have a positive correlation with physical practices as both data move in tandem over the years. China and India report highest death cases every year which stand out from the rest with a huge distinct difference. A relatively high death cases can also be identified in Russia and United State, with Russia having slow improvement and United State having minimal deterioration on their public health awareness. Northern Mariana Islands and Greenland generally remain their low death counts primarily due to their smaller population.

Risk factors for each category

In both categories, Europe is the only region with decreasing trend in number of deaths reported over years. From diet practices perspective, a diet low in nuts and seeds surpasses other diet practices and causes most deaths in all regions. Contrarily, in the physical practices category, every physical practice has different impacts on each region and there is no specific one with the worst consequence in all regions.
